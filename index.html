Overview

Crapette, also known as Russian Bank, is a competitive patience card game for two players. The goal of Crapette is to be the first player to play all of his cards

Setup

  1. Shuffle your deck and deal 13 cards face down as your reserve pile, with the top card face up.
  2. Place the remaining 39 cards face down as your stock pile.
  3. Deal four cards face up next to your reserve pile as your tableau.

Rules

On his turn, a player shall play a playable visible card according to the following prioritized rules:

The playable cards are:

  • The last card of each tableau row
  • The visible reserve card, or, if the reserve is empty, the visible waste card
  • The visible top talon card

Rule 1: Grow Foundation

If a card can be put on Foundation, then this is the top priority.

Rule 2: Crapotting

To crapot is to move a card on the opponent reserve or waste visible card.

The moving card shall be of the same suit, and be 1 value above or below:

  • If the reserve visible card is a 4♣️, opponent can put a 5♣️ or a 3♣️

Rule 3: Free Tableau Rows

Player can move cards in order to free a row.

Rule 4

If no prioritized move is possible and the top reserve card is hidden, then the player can flip and show it, then the rules above apply.

Rule 5

If no prioritized move is possible and the top stock card is hidden, then the player can flip and show it, then the rules above apply.

If no prioritized move is possible and the top stock card is visible, then the player can end his turn and place his top stock card on the waste pile.

If the player fails to perform a prioritized move, then the opponent can shout "Crapette" and explain the reason for the failure. The faulty player shall then cancel his last move or card flip and resign his turn to the opponent.

Winning

The first player to have no more cards in his reserve, stock, and waste is the winner.
